What Defines a Lunar Day (Tithi)?

Astronomical Definition: A lunar day (tithi) is the time it takes for the Moon to advance 12 degrees in longitude relative to the Sun.

Lunar Elongation = Moon's Longitude - Sun's Longitude
Each Tithi = 12° of elongation
30 Tithis = 360° = 1 Lunar Month

Key Points:

  • 🕐 Can end at any time of day or night
  • ⏱️ Duration varies from ~19 to ~26 hours
  • 🌍 Same worldwide (not tied to local sunrise)
  • 📐 Based on precise astronomical calculations
